# Reflection #1

Pod Members: **Esme, Frida, Melanie, Reyna**

## Reflection Questions

* Name at least one successful thing this week.

One successful thing this week was finalizing our product identity with both a name and logo. We had been debating this for a while, so it felt rewarding to finally call our product something specific instead of just “an onboarding tool.” We also made strong technical progress by connecting the frontend to the backend, aligning how documents will be stored, and adding Google sign-in as an authentication option.

* What were some challenges you and/or your group faced this week?

One challenge we faced was staying aligned on what each person was building and making sure our work was both productive and aligned with mentor expectations. We also spent time working through architecture decisions, especially authentication and document storage. Authentication was initially unclear, but we eventually landed on a workable approach with Google sign-in. For storage, we went back and forth between options, then aligned on using Supabase for our current needs instead of building a separate S3 bucket workflow right now.

* Did you finish all of your tasks in your sprint plan for this week? If you did not finish all of the planned tasks, how would you prioritize the remaining tasks on your list?  (i.e over planned, did not know how to implement certain features, miscommunication from the team, had to pivot from original plans, etc.)

Yes, we completed all of our planned tasks this sprint, and we also made progress beyond our original list. Our team process has been to identify tasks together, then divide work based on each person’s interest and ownership area. We do not overcommit to rigid daily deadlines; instead, we work day by day, adjust based on progress, and keep communication open so we can stay productive and continue moving forward.

* Did the resources provided to you help prepare you in planning and executing your capstone project sprint this week? Be specific, what resources did you find particularly helpful or which tasks did you need more support on?

Yes, the provided resources were very helpful for planning and execution. The project planning materials gave us structure, and the API/data model guidance helped us make clearer technical decisions. We also regularly reached out to our advisors whenever we needed clarification or direction, which helped us stay aligned and move forward confidently.

* Which features and user stories would you consider “at risk”? How will you change your plan if those items remain “at risk”?

At this stage, we do not consider any core MVP features to be critically at risk. We have made strong progress on the manager-facing experience and intentionally focused there first to establish the upload, quiz generation, and content-management foundation. Our next focus is the new-hire experience, and we will keep that scope tight to maintain momentum and avoid overextending the sprint.
